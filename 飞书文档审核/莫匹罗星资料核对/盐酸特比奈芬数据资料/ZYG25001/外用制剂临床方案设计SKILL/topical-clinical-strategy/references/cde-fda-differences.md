# CDE vs FDA Differences

## Purpose

Use this file when the user wants a China-only, FDA-only, or dual-region plan and you need to explain why the two paths diverge.

## 1. Overall Starting Point

### China

- More explicitly organized around registration category and the requirement to demonstrate clear clinical advantage for improved new drugs.
- Strong emphasis on whether the development plan matches the stated improvement goal.

### FDA

- More operationally shaped by pathway choice, public-review precedent, and detailed discussion of what bridge or safety package is acceptable.
- Public review packages often reveal how FDA actually treated max-use PK, long-term safety, and endpoint acceptability.

## 2. Improved New Drug Logic

### China

- “改良型新药” is a central formal framing.
- Clinical-advantage logic should be explicit and should drive the study design.

### FDA

- The same project may be described more through pathway and bridge logic than through a direct “improved new drug” label.
- The practical question is often whether the product fits 505(b)(2), what can be borrowed, and what new evidence is still needed.

## 3. Systemic Exposure and Clinical Pharmacology

### China

- If systemic exposure exists, clinical pharmacology may become a key research area rather than a small supportive package.
- Exposure-response logic can be especially helpful when strength or frequency rationale is weak.

### FDA

- Max-use PK / MUsT logic is often prominent in topical review practice.
- FDA may accept reduced downstream burden if the exposure bridge is strong and exposure remains low relative to known products.

## 4. Long-Term Safety

### China

- Long-term safety expectations should be justified through indication context, risk profile, and total development logic.

### FDA

- Public reviews often show indication-specific practical expectations, especially in chronic conditions.
- FDA may resist long-term-safety waivers even when early PK burden is light, depending on disease context.

## 5. Endpoint Culture

### China

- Guidance tends to emphasize overall scientific rationale and fit between endpoint, disease, and development goal.

### FDA

- Public review packages show strong attention to endpoint operationalization, statistical control, missing-data methods, and image-based or assessor-based endpoint acceptability.

## 6. Practical Output Rule

When both regions are in scope:

- anchor the China path on clinical-advantage and category logic
- anchor the FDA path on pathway, bridge, and review-practice logic
- then explicitly state where one region drives the conservative path and where the other region allows a more aggressive option
