# Failure Patterns

## Purpose

Use this file as a final sanity check before delivering a strategy. It helps prevent answers that sound confident but are structurally wrong.

## Global Failure Patterns

### 1. Pathway confusion

Common mistake:

- treating a product as if PSG or ANDA logic fully answers a 505(b)(2) or innovative-development question

Fix:

- separate pathway choice from technical support logic

### 2. Over-trusting oral precedent

Common mistake:

- assuming that known oral efficacy or safety automatically solves topical development strategy

Fix:

- still ask how local delivery, local tolerability, and systemic exposure differ after topical reformulation

### 3. Ignoring concentration rationale

Common mistake:

- pretending the proposed strength is obviously acceptable when no topical precedent exists

Fix:

- treat weak strength rationale as a non-blocking but material uncertainty
- make the conservative path more exploration-heavy

### 4. Under-specifying local safety

Common mistake:

- focusing on efficacy and systemic PK while glossing over irritation, sensitization, or local tolerability

Fix:

- always ask whether the product’s value proposition could be damaged by local safety findings

### 5. False precision

Common mistake:

- stating exact study counts or exact sample sizes without sufficient basis

Fix:

- describe the likely range and the assumption that would determine the final design

## Region-Specific Failure Patterns

### China

Common mistake:

- discussing a改良型新药项目 without clearly defining the clinical advantage being claimed

Fix:

- explicitly state whether the claimed advantage is better efficacy, better safety, better adherence, or lower systemic exposure

### FDA

Common mistake:

- borrowing an individual review case as if it were a formal rule

Fix:

- explain when a case is only a precedent signal and not a binding expectation

## Indication-Specific Failure Patterns

### Acne

- forgetting component-contribution issues in combinations
- underestimating adolescent-development implications
- over-copying endpoint packages without checking whether the product is really trying to establish the same claim

### Rosacea

- mixing papulopustular disease with persistent erythema logic
- treating chronic-use safety as optional too early
- failing to distinguish disease flare from treatment reaction

### Superficial Fungal Infection

- collapsing mycological cure and clinical cure into one vague result
- ignoring follow-up timing
- assuming short treatment duration can be justified without stronger evidence

### AGA

- applying short dermatology development timelines to a long-horizon disease
- using soft photography endpoints without enough standardization
- downplaying systemic or transfer exposure for hormone-pathway products

## Final Pre-Answer Checklist

Before answering, check:

- Did I distinguish innovative vs improved vs generic-like logic?
- Did I separate formal guidance from case-derived practice?
- Did I explain why the conservative and aggressive paths differ?
- Did I say what evidence gap most changes the recommendation?
- Did I overstate PSG?
- Did I make any claim that sounds more certain than the evidence supports?
