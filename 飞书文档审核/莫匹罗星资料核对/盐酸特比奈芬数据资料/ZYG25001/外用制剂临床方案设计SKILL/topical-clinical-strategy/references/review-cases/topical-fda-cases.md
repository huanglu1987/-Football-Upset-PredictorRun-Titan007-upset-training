# Topical FDA Review Cases

## Purpose

Use these cases to understand practical FDA acceptance patterns for topical products. Do not treat them as binding rules.

## 1. Hyftor

Official review:

- `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2022/213478Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`

Why it matters:

- same active ingredient already known systemically
- topical product with clear systemic-exposure bridge questions

Practical takeaways:

- FDA accepted a bridge strategy comparing topical exposure with oral sirolimus exposure logic.
- Max-use or relative BA logic can be central when a known systemic active is reformulated as a topical product.
- Endpoint acceptability is not just about whether the endpoint improved; FDA scrutinized whether image-based composite measures were sufficiently validated for regulatory decision-making.

## 2. Zilxi

Official review:

- `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2020/213690Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`

Why it matters:

- topical rosacea product with max-use PK and long-term safety themes

Practical takeaways:

- Max-use PK can meaningfully reduce the need for heavier systemic-clinical-pharmacology work when exposure remains low.
- FDA still paid close attention to long-term safety expectations because rosacea is chronic.
- Public review supports the practical importance of co-primary structure and prespecified missing-data strategy.

## 3. Epsolay

Official review:

- `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2023/214510Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`

Why it matters:

- rosacea program where local tolerability and long-term safety were important

Practical takeaways:

- FDA may allow lighter PK burden when systemic exposure is biologically trivial and well justified.
- Even with low PK concern, long-term safety may still remain important in chronic dermatology settings.
- The review illustrates how FDA differentiates endpoint structure, sensitivity analyses, and tolerability interpretation.

## 4. Twyneo

Official review:

- `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2021/214902Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`

Why it matters:

- acne product with bridge logic and pediatric considerations

Practical takeaways:

- Max-use PK and BA bridge work can be used to support reliance on existing knowledge for a known active.
- FDA still scrutinized pediatric exposure adequacy and application conditions.
- This case is useful when the value proposition is formulation optimization rather than a wholly new efficacy concept.

## 5. Cabtreo

Official review:

- `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2024/216632Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`

Why it matters:

- acne triple-combination product with contribution and bridge issues

Practical takeaways:

- Combination logic can trigger a contribution question even when all actives are familiar.
- Public review supports the importance of max-use PK bridge work plus component-contribution evidence.
- Combination convenience does not eliminate the need to justify why each component belongs in the final fixed product.

## 6. AGA Note: Topical Finasteride

Official FDA safety communication:

- `https://www.fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding/fda-alerts-health-care-providers-compounders-and-consumers-potential-risks-associated-compounded`

Why it matters:

- there is no FDA-approved topical finasteride product to anchor review-case borrowing

Practical takeaways:

- AGA strategy involving topical finasteride should not pretend there is a mature FDA-approved topical precedent.
- FDA has already highlighted systemic adverse-event concern, local reaction concern, and transfer-exposure concern.
- For this type of product, the conservative path should usually keep exposure and safety characterization in the foreground.
