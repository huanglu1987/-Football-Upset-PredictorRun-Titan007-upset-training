# AGA Module

## Scope

Use this module for androgenetic alopecia strategy work. Treat it as a high-value but higher-uncertainty module relative to acne or rosacea.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- official FDA labeling and public materials for relevant hair-loss products
- current FDA safety communications if the product raises systemic-exposure concerns
- China local-acting topical and exposure-response guidance

If the product involves topical finasteride-like logic, live official browsing is especially important.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors:

- target-area hair count
- standardized photography or phototrichogram
- investigator global assessment
- patient self-assessment

Time horizon is longer than the acne, rosacea, or superficial fungal modules.

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- local vs systemic exposure balance
- exposure-response analysis
- long-term treatment horizon
- transfer exposure and reproductive-risk communication if relevant
- adequacy of objective hair-assessment methods

## Confirmatory Pattern

Typical strategy themes:

- longer treatment duration
- stronger dependence on standardized imaging methods
- careful dose and frequency selection
- higher value for PK and exposure-response reasoning than many other topical modules

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- systemic exposure is uncertain
- the active has meaningful systemic class risks
- the objective hair-assessment method is not fully locked
- the product uses a novel concentration with limited external support

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- a strong local-delivery rationale exists
- systemic exposure is convincingly low
- early biologic or clinical signal is already available
- the main question is optimization rather than proof of concept

## Common Failure Points

- applying short dermatology timelines to a long-horizon hair-loss program
- relying on soft or poorly standardized photography
- understating systemic-exposure and secondary-contact concerns for hormone-pathway products
