# Alopecia Areata Module

## Scope

Use this module for skin-topical alopecia areata strategy work.

Start by separating:

- localized or patchy alopecia areata versus extensive scalp involvement
- scalp-only disease versus eyebrow, beard, or other hair-bearing areas
- adult-first versus adolescent expansion
- immunologic proof-of-concept ambition versus known-topical optimization

Do not merge alopecia areata logic with androgenetic alopecia logic. The hair endpoints may overlap, but the disease biology, variability, and treatment goals do not.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- FDA AA disease context from approved systemic products when background regulatory expectations matter:
  - `https://www.fda.gov/drugs/development-approval-process-drugs/drug-trials-snapshots-litfulo`
- ClinicalTrials.gov topical or localized-AA precedent:
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05803070`
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06087796`
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03354637`
- China local-acting topical guidance
- China innovation-method and exposure-response guidance when the program is mechanism-led rather than bridge-led

At present, this skill does not embed a disease-specific CDE or FDA topical AA guidance file. Strategy therefore depends more heavily on mechanism logic, public registry precedent, and general topical innovation rules.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors may include:

- SALT-based scalp hair response metrics
- patch-level regrowth measures for localized disease
- standardized photography or dermoscopic assessment
- investigator and patient global assessments as supportive endpoints

Pay attention to whether the product is trying to claim:

- localized patch regrowth
- broader scalp benefit
- steroid-sparing or nonsteroidal convenience value

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- disease heterogeneity and spontaneous fluctuation
- lesion-selection rules and background treatment control
- objective hair assessment quality
- whether local signal can be read over a short exploratory period
- whether patient-first early work is more informative than healthy-volunteer work

In many AA programs, healthy volunteers have limited PD value. If systemic exposure risk is manageable, patient-first or very early patient repeat-dose work may be more decision-relevant than a prolonged healthy-volunteer package.

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common strategy themes:

- localized disease may allow smaller exploratory signal studies
- broader scalp claims will need more rigorous objective hair endpoints and longer follow-up
- comparator logic often depends on whether the project is positioned against topical corticosteroid standard practice or as a novel nonsteroidal option

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the mechanism is novel
- the target population extends beyond localized patchy disease
- spontaneous remission or relapse may confound signal reading
- objective assessment methods are not fully standardized
- exposure or long-term local safety is still uncertain

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- the product is limited to localized patchy AA
- the local delivery rationale is strong
- early patient signal can be captured with dermoscopy or patch-level response methods
- the development claim is narrower and avoids overreaching into extensive disease

## Common Failure Points

- reusing AGA timelines or endpoint logic without adjustment
- ignoring spontaneous fluctuation and regression-to-the-mean risk
- starting with too broad a disease-severity range
- weak lesion-selection and photography standardization
