# Vitiligo Module

## Scope

Use this module for skin-topical vitiligo strategy work.

Start by separating:

- nonsegmental vitiligo versus segmental vitiligo
- facial repigmentation ambition versus broader body-area ambition
- induction of repigmentation versus maintenance of repigmentation
- adult-first versus adolescent expansion

Do not assume all vitiligo programs can use the same body-surface-area limits, treatment duration, or endpoint package.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors may include:

- facial Vitiligo Area Scoring Index style endpoints
- total-body vitiligo area scoring endpoints when the intended claim goes beyond the face
- investigator global or patient-reported cosmetic acceptability only as supportive rather than primary anchors

Pay attention to whether the product is trying to claim:

- facial repigmentation first
- broader nonfacial repigmentation
- maintenance after prior repigmentation
- combination value with phototherapy

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- treatment duration realism, because repigmentation usually needs longer observation
- body-surface-area cap and treated-area exposure logic
- face versus nonfacial response differences
- need for patient PK or max-use PK if treated area expands
- phototherapy combination plans and timing
- adolescent inclusion if the intended population starts at age 12 or older

For mechanism classes with systemic immunologic concern, do not let the topical route erase the need for exposure control.

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common strategy themes:

- randomized, vehicle-controlled studies
- treatment periods long enough to detect clinically meaningful repigmentation
- face-specific primary endpoints when facial cosmetic benefit is central
- extension periods or longer follow-up to understand durability and continued response

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the mechanism is novel for vitiligo topical use
- the intended claim extends beyond facial repigmentation
- treated body-surface-area may exceed a narrow exposure-controlled use case
- the program wants maintenance or combination positioning early
- the exposure package is still immature

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- the mechanism already has close topical precedent
- exposure is well characterized under proposed treated-area limits
- the target claim is narrower, such as nonsegmental vitiligo with strong facial focus
- the early package already supports likely BID or chronic-use feasibility

## Common Failure Points

- treating vitiligo like a short-horizon inflammatory disease
- failing to separate facial and total-body expectations
- over-claiming cosmetic benefit without robust repigmentation endpoints
- expanding body-surface-area ambition before exposure control is mature
