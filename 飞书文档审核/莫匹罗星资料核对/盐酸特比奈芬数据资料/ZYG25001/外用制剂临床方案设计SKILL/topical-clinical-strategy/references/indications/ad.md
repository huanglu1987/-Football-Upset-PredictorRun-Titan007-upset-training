# Atopic Dermatitis Module

## Scope

Use this module for skin-topical atopic dermatitis strategy work.

Start by separating:

- mild-to-moderate disease that is realistically topical-led
- broader moderate disease where systemic-exposure or rescue-treatment issues may change the program
- pediatric expansion versus adult-first development

Do not assume all AD topical programs follow the same duration or endpoint package.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- FDA public review materials for topical AD products such as:
  - Eucrisa summary and medical reviews: `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2016/207695Orig1s000SumR.pdf`
  - Opzelura multidisciplinary review: `https://www.fda.gov/media/154307/download`
- FDA pediatric timing guidance for AD: `https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/atopic-dermatitis-timing-pediatric-studies-during-development-systemic-drugs`
- China local-acting topical guidance
- China exposure-response and innovation clinical pharmacology guidance when the product is more innovative

At present, this skill does not embed a disease-specific CDE AD trial guidance file. China-side AD strategy should therefore be anchored first in the general local-acting, exposure-response, and innovation or improved-drug rules.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors may include:

- IGA or vIGA-AD success
- extent or severity measures such as EASI or BSA, when the intended claim supports them
- itch improvement measures when pruritus relief is a core value proposition

Pay attention to whether the product is trying to claim:

- lesion improvement only
- itch benefit
- steroid-sparing or nonsteroidal chronic-use value

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- stinging, burning, and local tolerability
- need for maximal-use PK or patient PK when systemic-exposure concern exists
- how rescue medication use will be handled
- pediatric timing and age de-escalation logic
- whether local efficacy signal and itch signal emerge together or not

For mechanism classes with systemic or immunologic concern, do not let “topical” automatically collapse the early clinical pharmacology package.

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common strategy themes:

- adequate and well-controlled vehicle-based efficacy trials
- carefully prespecified disease-severity entry criteria
- itch and global-assessment logic that matches the label ambition
- a chronic-use or longer-term safety layer when long-term use is part of the intended positioning

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the mechanism is novel for AD topical use
- systemic exposure is uncertain or class risks are material
- pediatric inclusion is early and ambitious
- the product aims to support broader or chronic-use positioning
- the itch claim is important but early itch signal is not yet clean

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- local delivery is convincing and systemic exposure is very low
- the product improves convenience, tolerability, or adherence of a known topical logic
- precedent from FDA public reviews is close to the current age group and disease-severity target
- early local PK or PK/PD reasoning already helps narrow strength and frequency

## Common Failure Points

- treating AD as a single homogeneous severity space
- ignoring itch as a development-defining endpoint issue
- underestimating chronic-use safety just because the route is topical
- expanding into children before adult exposure and tolerability are mature enough
