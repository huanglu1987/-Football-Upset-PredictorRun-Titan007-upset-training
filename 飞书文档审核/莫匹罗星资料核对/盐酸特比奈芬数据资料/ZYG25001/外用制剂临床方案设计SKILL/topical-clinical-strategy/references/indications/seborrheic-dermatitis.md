# Seborrheic Dermatitis Module

## Scope

Use this module for adolescent and adult seborrheic dermatitis strategy work.

Start by separating:

- scalp-predominant disease
- face or trunk involvement
- chronic relapsing control versus short-course flare control

Do not automatically merge infant cradle cap into the same program logic.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- Zoryve roflumilast foam multidisciplinary review for seborrheic dermatitis: `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2024/217242Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`
- current FDA labeling for Zoryve foam, 0.3%
- China local-acting topical guidance
- relevant same-class or same-target ClinicalTrials.gov patterns when relapse-control design questions remain open

At present, this skill does not embed a disease-specific CDE seborrheic dermatitis trial guidance file. China-side design therefore depends more heavily on the general local-acting framework plus precedent interpretation.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors may include:

- investigator global severity success
- erythema and scaling improvement
- itch improvement when symptomatic burden is material

Because seborrheic dermatitis is chronic and relapsing, also define whether the program is trying to support:

- acute improvement only
- relapse control
- longer-term intermittent or maintenance use

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- scalp and hair-bearing area usability
- face-site tolerability
- chronic relapsing use pattern
- maximal-use or patient PK if large surface areas or systemic-exposure concerns exist
- whether adolescent inclusion is planned early

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common strategy themes:

- vehicle-controlled efficacy trials
- treatment periods long enough to capture clinically meaningful scaling and erythema improvement
- secondary itch analysis when symptoms justify it
- longer-term safety or extension support when chronic-use positioning matters

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the product seeks chronic maintenance language
- scalp, face, and trunk use all need to be covered
- adolescent inclusion is important
- systemic exposure or long-term tolerability is still uncertain
- the program is trying to move beyond standard antifungal or steroid logic

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- the product mainly optimizes delivery to common seborrheic dermatitis sites
- systemic exposure is convincingly low
- precedent from recent FDA public review logic is close to the intended claim
- the value proposition is fast practical control rather than broad novel efficacy ambition

## Common Failure Points

- treating infant cradle cap and adolescent or adult seborrheic dermatitis as one development problem
- underestimating relapse and chronic-use questions
- borrowing fungal-disease cure logic instead of dermatitis-control logic
- ignoring site usability in hair-bearing regions
