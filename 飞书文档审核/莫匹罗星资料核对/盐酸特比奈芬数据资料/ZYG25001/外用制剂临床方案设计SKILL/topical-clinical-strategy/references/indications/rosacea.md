# Rosacea Module

## Scope

Use this module first for papulopustular rosacea. Do not mix persistent erythema and papulopustular disease unless the user explicitly wants a broader program.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- relevant FDA public reviews for rosacea topical products
- the local `505(b)(2)` case CSV
- China local-acting topical guidance for general principles

High-value FDA case anchors include products such as Zilxi and Epsolay.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors:

- inflammatory lesion absolute change
- IGA success rate

Long-term safety often matters more than in acne because rosacea is chronic and recurrent.

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- facial sensitivity and irritation
- how to separate disease worsening from treatment reaction
- whether long-term safety will be expected
- whether systemic exposure is trivial enough to simplify the clinical pharmacology package

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common confirmatory themes:

- vehicle-controlled trials
- carefully prespecified missing-data and multiplicity methods
- a longer-term safety extension when the disease context supports it

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the product uses a new delivery approach
- long-term tolerability is uncertain
- the target population includes older or more sensitive patients
- the local safety story is not yet mature

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- systemic exposure is very low
- the product mainly optimizes delivery or tolerability of a known active
- precedent from public reviews closely matches the current program

## Common Failure Points

- underestimating the importance of chronic-use safety
- assuming facial irritation findings are minor operational issues
- borrowing an endpoint structure without checking whether the disease subtype actually matches
