# Psoriasis Module

## Scope

Use this module first for topical plaque psoriasis strategy work.

Start by separating:

- limited-BSA plaque psoriasis that is clearly topical-manageable
- scalp, intertriginous, or other difficult-site disease
- broader disease burden that could increase systemic-exposure, adherence, or rescue-treatment complexity

Do not borrow systemic psoriasis development logic unless the user is clearly asking for a mixed topical-plus-systemic program.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- FDA public review materials for topical psoriasis products such as:
  - Vtama multidisciplinary review: `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2022/215272Orig1s000MultidisciplineR.pdf`
  - Zoryve foam supplemental review for scalp/body psoriasis: `https://www.fda.gov/media/185434/download`
- FDA public labeling and review materials for current topical plaque psoriasis products
- China local-acting topical guidance
- China exposure-response guidance when dose and frequency selection remain open

At present, this skill does not embed a disease-specific CDE psoriasis trial guidance file. China-side reasoning should therefore stay anchored in the general local-acting and registration-path framework first.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors may include:

- PGA or equivalent global severity success
- target lesion or region-specific improvement
- itch improvement when relevant
- additional extent or severity measures when the intended claim requires them

The endpoint package should reflect whether the claim is for:

- general plaque psoriasis
- scalp disease
- special-site disease
- a broader maintenance or remission-oriented value story

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- local irritation or contact dermatitis
- systemic exposure under larger-BSA or difficult-site use
- strength and frequency selection
- whether maximal-use PK is needed
- whether the product is trying to support remission durability or just acute control

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common strategy themes:

- vehicle-controlled efficacy trials
- treatment periods often long enough to capture a credible plaque response signal
- open-label or extension safety support when longer-term use is expected
- careful handling of site-specific disease heterogeneity

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the mechanism is novel in topical psoriasis
- the intended population has broader BSA involvement
- the product seeks a stronger maintenance or remission positioning
- systemic exposure or special-site tolerability is not yet settled
- early data suggest irritancy or contact dermatitis could affect usability

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- the product targets a limited topical-treatment population
- systemic exposure is convincingly low
- the main value is better tolerability, site fit, or ease of use relative to known topical options
- precedent from recent FDA topical psoriasis approvals is close to the proposed program

## Common Failure Points

- importing systemic-program expectations into a topical program
- ignoring difficult-site and scalp-specific operational issues
- underestimating contact dermatitis, folliculitis, or local tolerability as path-defining problems
- assuming a broad chronic-use claim can be supported without enough long-term evidence
