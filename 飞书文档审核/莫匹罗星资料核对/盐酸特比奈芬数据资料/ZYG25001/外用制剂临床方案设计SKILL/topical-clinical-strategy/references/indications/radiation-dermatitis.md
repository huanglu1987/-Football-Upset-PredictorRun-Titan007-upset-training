# Radiation Dermatitis Module

## Scope

Use this module for skin-topical radiation dermatitis strategy work.

Start by separating:

- prevention versus treatment
- breast radiotherapy versus head-and-neck or other irradiation fields
- mild to moderate dermatitis control versus severe-dermatitis reduction
- supportive-care positioning versus novel anti-inflammatory or wound-healing mechanism positioning

Do not assume prevention and treatment can share one endpoint package or one study architecture.

## Priority Sources

Read:

- ClinicalTrials.gov precedent:
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04173247`
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05190770`
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05269160`
  - `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07192588`
- China local-acting topical guidance
- China clinical-entry and exposure-response guidance when the product is more than supportive-care optimization

At present, this skill does not embed a disease-specific CDE or FDA radiation-dermatitis drug-development guidance file. Strategy should therefore rely on study-setting logic, registry precedent, and general topical-development rules.

## Core Endpoint Logic

Common efficacy anchors may include:

- incidence of radiation dermatitis above a defined grade
- time to onset of clinically meaningful dermatitis
- worst grade over treatment course
- symptom burden such as pain, burning, or pruritus
- healing or resolution time when positioned as treatment rather than prevention

Pay attention to whether the product is trying to claim:

- prevention of dermatitis
- reduction in maximum severity
- faster healing once dermatitis occurs
- additive value to standard supportive care

## Early-Stage Focus

Pay special attention to:

- timing relative to radiotherapy start and duration
- compatibility with standard skin-care or topical steroid background care
- irradiated-field tolerability
- whether the program needs prevention-only or treatment-only proof first
- site differences such as breast versus head-and-neck skin burden

Healthy volunteers may have limited relevance for pharmacology here. If systemic exposure risk is low and local tolerability under irradiated-skin conditions is the main question, early patient-setting work is often more informative than a long healthy-volunteer package.

## Confirmatory Pattern

Common strategy themes:

- randomized controlled supportive-care studies
- endpoint definitions tied to CTCAE-like grading or similarly standardized grading systems
- radiotherapy-setting integration, including start timing and concomitant standard care
- shorter treatment horizon than AGA or vitiligo, but stronger operational dependence on oncology workflow

## Conservative Path Triggers

Lean conservative when:

- the claim combines prevention and treatment in one program
- the mechanism is novel in irradiated skin
- multiple irradiation settings are being combined too early
- background standard-of-care variability is high

## Aggressive Path Triggers

Lean aggressive when:

- the product is positioned narrowly, such as prevention in one radiotherapy setting
- standard background care is well controlled
- local tolerability risk is manageable
- operational integration with radiotherapy workflow is already mature

## Common Failure Points

- mixing prevention and treatment populations
- weak control of concomitant supportive care
- endpoint drift across oncology sites
- underestimating field-specific differences in dermatitis burden
