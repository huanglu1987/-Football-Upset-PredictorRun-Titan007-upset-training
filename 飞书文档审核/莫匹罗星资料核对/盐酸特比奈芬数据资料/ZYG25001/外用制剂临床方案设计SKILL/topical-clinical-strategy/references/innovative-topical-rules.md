# Innovative Topical Drug Rules

## Purpose

Use this file when the project is best judged as a true innovative topical drug program, or when the product is still path-uncertain but clearly cannot be handled only with improved-new-drug logic.

This file is designed to make the current skill more useful for external topical innovation programs, not just improved topical products.

## Read This File When

Read this file when one or more of the following is true:

- the active ingredient has no meaningful approved-topical precedent
- the product relies on a novel mechanism or novel target for the skin disease
- the formulation creates a new local-delivery or exposure profile
- the early program needs true proof-of-concept reasoning rather than only bridge reasoning
- the product may require a more formal early clinical pharmacology package

## Core Distinction From Improved-New-Drug Logic

For improved-new-drug projects, the central question is often:

- what can be borrowed
- what can be bridged
- what clinical advantage must be shown

For innovative-topical projects, the central question becomes:

- how to establish credible human proof of pharmacology
- how to justify concentration, frequency, and regimen
- how to manage local and systemic uncertainty before confirmatory development

Do not force a true innovative program into a bridge-heavy answer if the scientific problem is still proof of concept.

## Early Clinical Development Logic

### 1. First-in-human and first-patient strategy

Do not assume a classical healthy-volunteer SAD or MAD package is always the default.

Instead, decide among:

- healthy-volunteer early PK or tolerability work
- patient-first early PK or PK/PD work
- maximal-use PK integrated with local safety and exploratory activity
- mixed designs that combine exposure, tolerability, and early signal

Choice depends on:

- predicted systemic exposure
- local tolerability risk
- feasibility of pharmacodynamic readouts
- target disease severity and ethical fit for patient-first work

### 2. Strength and frequency selection

In innovative topical programs, concentration choice often needs more work than in improved programs.

Use:

- nonclinical translational logic
- local PK or systemic PK where informative
- exposure-response analysis
- early dose-ranging or frequency-ranging evidence

Weak strength rationale should move the answer toward a more exploration-heavy conservative path, not toward a hard stop.

### 3. Local and systemic safety package

Early safety planning should actively assess:

- local irritation
- sensitization
- phototoxicity or photoallergy when relevant
- systemic exposure under maximal or realistic use
- class-specific risks if the mechanism raises immunologic, hormonal, or reproductive concerns

For some innovative topical programs, the route is local but the safety reasoning still needs to look more like a formal innovation package than a simple topical bridge package.

## Proof-of-Concept Logic

Before recommending an aggressive path, ask whether the program has enough evidence to answer these questions:

- Is there human evidence of biologic or clinical activity?
- Is the proposed strength defensible?
- Is dosing frequency reasonably justified?
- Is local benefit meaningfully separated from local tolerability burden?

If not, the skill should explicitly recommend a proof-of-concept-oriented early package rather than pretending the program is ready for direct confirmatory compression.

## Transition To Exploratory and Confirmatory Development

The move from early work into P2 or pivotal-like work should be tied to:

- exposure confidence
- concentration confidence
- local tolerability maturity
- clarity of disease-specific endpoint behavior
- adequacy of the intended chronic-use safety strategy

Avoid false precision on study count. Instead, explain the evidentiary strength likely needed for the target claim and region.

## Region-Specific Emphasis

China emphasis:

- overall development logic and clinical-entry readiness
- stronger attention to whether the total evidence package supports the next clinical step
- for innovative drugs, clinical pharmacology planning should be integrated early rather than treated as an afterthought

FDA emphasis:

- whether the topical route truly reduces or contains systemic concern
- whether maximal-use PK or related exposure work is sufficient
- whether the endpoint and duration logic are aligned with recent review practice
- whether the program has enough data to justify any compressed or innovative design choice

## Conservative Path For Innovative Topical Drugs

Lean conservative when:

- no meaningful human exposure data exist
- the target strength is weakly justified
- the mechanism has systemic or class risks
- the disease requires longer-term treatment or relapse control
- local PD or early clinical signal is still immature

Typical conservative features:

- fuller early clinical pharmacology
- explicit dose or frequency exploration
- stronger maximal-use PK logic
- longer safety maturation before confirmatory claims

## Aggressive Path For Innovative Topical Drugs

Lean aggressive only when:

- local-delivery rationale is strong
- human exposure information is already informative
- early pharmacology or clinical signal is credible
- the indication module supports an endpoint package with relatively clear operational precedent

Typical aggressive features:

- integrated early PK plus signal designs
- narrower dose exploration
- faster movement into proof-of-concept or pivotal-like work

But always mark the regulatory risk increase explicitly.

## Common Failure Points

- forcing innovative programs into improved-drug bridge language
- assuming topical route erases the need for serious clinical pharmacology planning
- skipping rigorous strength and frequency justification
- over-compressing development before human proof of concept is mature
