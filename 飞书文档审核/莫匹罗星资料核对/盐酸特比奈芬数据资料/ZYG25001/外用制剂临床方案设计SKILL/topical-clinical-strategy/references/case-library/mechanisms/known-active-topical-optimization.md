# Mechanism Family: Known-Active Topical Optimization

## Use This Family For

- known active ingredient with topical precedent
- new dosage form or formulation intended to improve tolerability, adherence, convenience, or site usability
- improved new-drug programs where bridge and clinical-advantage logic matter more than true proof of concept

## Closest Current Examples

- `worked-examples/minoxidil-optimization-dual-region.md`
- `worked-examples/seborrheic-dermatitis-foam-dual-region.md`

## Core Development Question

The central question is usually not:

- does the active ingredient work at all

It is more often:

- does the new product deliver a defendable clinical advantage

## Typical Early Package

- limited single-use risk reduction only if the new dosage form changes exposure uncertainty
- stronger emphasis on repeat-dose local tolerability
- earlier movement into patient usability or short exploratory efficacy work

## Best Fit Claims

- less irritation
- better adherence
- easier scalp, face, or hair-bearing-site use
- improved user experience without loss of expected benefit

## Common Failure Points

- treating an optimization program like a new molecular entity
- claiming multiple advantages without study design alignment
- under-documenting why the new dosage form is clinically meaningful
