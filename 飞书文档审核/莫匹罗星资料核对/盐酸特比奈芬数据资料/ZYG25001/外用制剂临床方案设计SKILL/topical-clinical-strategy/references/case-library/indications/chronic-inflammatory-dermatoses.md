# Case Family: Chronic Inflammatory Dermatoses

## Use This Family For

- atopic dermatitis
- seborrheic dermatitis
- plaque psoriasis
- other chronic relapsing inflammatory skin diseases

## Main Sub-Families

### 1. 创新抗炎外用型

Closest current example:

- `worked-examples/ad-innovative-dual-region.md`

Core question:

- how to earn human proof of concept while still controlling local and systemic uncertainty

### 2. 已知逻辑剂型优化型

Closest current example:

- `worked-examples/seborrheic-dermatitis-foam-dual-region.md`

Core question:

- whether the dosage form improves site usability, speed of control, or adherence enough to justify an improved-drug path

## Shared Strategic Questions

- is the program aiming at acute control, relapse control, or maintenance
- how much chronic-use safety is needed
- whether symptom endpoints such as itch matter as much as morphology
- whether face, scalp, body, or special-site usability changes the design

## Typical Early-Study Split

- innovative family:
  - often `healthy volunteers to patients`
  - stronger need for PK, patient tolerability, and early signal integration
- optimization family:
  - early work may move faster into repeat-dose patient usability and local tolerability

## Fast Routing Rule

If the product relies on a novel anti-inflammatory mechanism, start from the innovative family.

If the product mainly changes delivery, site fit, or convenience, start from the optimization family.

## Common Failure Points

- forgetting to define acute versus maintenance ambition
- underestimating chronic-use safety
- treating all inflammatory dermatoses as having the same endpoint and duration logic
