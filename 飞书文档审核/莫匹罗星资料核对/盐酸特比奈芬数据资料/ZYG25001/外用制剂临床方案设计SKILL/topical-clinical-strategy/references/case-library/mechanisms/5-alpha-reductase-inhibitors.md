# Mechanism Family: 5-Alpha Reductase Inhibitors

## Use This Family For

- topical finasteride
- topical dutasteride
- other topical programs whose main risk logic is DHT suppression and hormone-pathway exposure

## Why This Family Needs Its Own Card

These projects are not just “AGA projects”.

They are defined by:

- systemic-exposure sensitivity
- endocrine and reproductive-risk concern
- chronic-use horizon
- the need to prove that local delivery meaningfully improves the benefit-risk profile

## Closest Current Example

- `worked-examples/aga-topical-finasteride-risk.md`

## How To Apply It To Dutasteride-Like Projects

Use the finasteride-risk example as the base logic, then strengthen:

- systemic PK attention
- DHT suppression monitoring
- repeat-dose accumulation concern
- conservative long-term safety framing

Because dutasteride-like logic may carry a stronger or longer systemic effect concern, do not simply reuse a minoxidil-style optimization path.

## Typical Recommended Early Package

- healthy male first-human PK and local tolerability, if justified
- then patient repeat-dose PK and tolerability
- only then broader efficacy exploration

## Claims That Need Strong Support

- reduced systemic burden versus oral route
- acceptable long-term safety for chronic use
- no unacceptable transfer or secondary-contact risk if relevant

## Common Failure Points

- jumping directly into long efficacy studies without enough PK control
- using convenience claims when the real regulatory question is systemic risk containment
- under-specifying endocrine-related monitoring
